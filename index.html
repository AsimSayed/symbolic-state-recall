The gap

Screen readers read math like prose.
That's the entire problem.

A sighted person sees x² + 3x + 5 = 20 as a spatial object. They can focus on the exponent, glance at one side of the equals sign, mentally isolate a term. A VoiceOver user hears a flat stream: "x squared plus 3 x plus 5 equals 20." No way in. No way to grab a piece.

Screen readers today

  • Reads equations linearly, left to right
  • No concept of "the part inside the integral"
  • Can't select or extract sub-expressions
  • User must retype from memory
  • Re-listen to entire equation for one piece

With SymbolicStateRecall

  • Navigate equations as a tree structure
  • Drill into any level: integral, fraction, exponent
  • Select and extract any node instantly
  • Spoken feedback at every navigation step
  • Insert sub-expression at cursor with one key

The journey

From flat audio
to structural navigation.

01 — The status quo

A wall of sound with no way in

Your screen reader announces: "x squared plus 3 x plus 5 equals 20." You heard it. You understood it. But now you need just the first term. You can't click on it. You can't select it. You either retype it from memory or re-listen to the whole thing. This is the daily reality for every blind student working with math.

LINEAR · NO STRUCTURE · NO SELECTION
02 — The parse

Text becomes structure

SSR's tokenizer breaks the equation into 7 tokens. The parser builds an Abstract Syntax Tree: an equation node at the root, left and right sides as children, individual terms as leaves. The flat string is now a navigable structure. Every node knows what it is — power, product, constant — and can describe itself in plain language.

7 TOKENS → 6 NODES → 1 AST
03 — The navigation

Three keys to any node

Press 1 — VoiceOver announces "Line 1, equation." Press L — "Left side, 3 items." Press 1 — "x squared." Three keystrokes. You're now pointing at exactly the sub-expression you need. Every step is spoken. Every state change is announced. You never have to guess where you are.

PATH: 1 → L → 1 · SPOKEN AT EVERY STEP
04 — The extraction

Space. Inserted. Done.

Press Space. The selected node is serialized back to plain text — x^2 — and inserted at your cursor position in Notes.app. You never left your text editor. You never touched the clipboard. The entire interaction was non-visual, structural, and took under two seconds.

x^2 → CURSOR · ZERO VISION REQUIRED
What it parses

Calculus-level math.
From plain text.

No LaTeX required. No MathML. SSR accepts the kind of math notation people actually type. Because the people who need this tool shouldn't have to learn a second notation system to study the subject they already understand.

Expressions

Equations, powers, fractions

Full operator precedence handling, implicit multiplication, multi-line equation support with tolerant parsing.

x^2 + 3x = 7  •  (x+1)/(x-1)  •  3x → 3*x
Calculus

Integrals, derivatives, limits

Definite and indefinite integrals, derivative notation, limit expressions with approach syntax.

int_0^1 x^2 dx  •  d/dx  •  lim_x->0
Functions

Trig, logarithms, roots

sin, cos, tan, ln, log, square roots, nth roots. All parsed into navigable tree nodes.

sin(x)  •  ln(e^x)  •  sqrt(x)  •  root3(8)
Resilience

Error-tolerant by design

Skips non-math lines. Preserves last valid path on error. 400ms multi-digit input buffering. Never drops you out of recall mode.
