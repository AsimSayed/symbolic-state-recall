The Problem

Screen readers weren't built for equations

Existing tools read math linearly, start to finish. For a long equation, that means listening to the entire expression just to find the part you need.

⌛

Time lost to sequential reading

A multi-term calculus expression can take 30+ seconds to listen through. If you need just the exponent, you still hear everything before it.

🧠

Cognitive overload

Holding an entire equation in working memory while searching for a specific term taxes cognitive load unnecessarily.

✏️

No way to extract parts

Even after locating a term, there's no standard way to grab just that piece and insert it into your own work.

How It Works

Four keystrokes to any part of any equation

Math Recall parses equations into a tree. You navigate the tree with single keystrokes. It speaks what it finds at every level.

Copy

Copy any equation from your textbook, editor, or notes. Math Recall watches the clipboard.

Trigger

Press Option + Space from any app. Math Recall activates globally.

Navigate

Type a path: line, side, item. Like 1 L 2 to reach the second term on the left of line 1.

Insert

Press Space to insert the selected term at your cursor. Press Esc to exit.

Features

Built for the way you actually work

Math Recall integrates with your existing tools and accessibility setup, not the other way around.

🎨

Tree-based navigation

Equations are parsed into an abstract syntax tree. Jump directly to fractions, exponents, integrands, or any nested structure.

🗣

VoiceOver integration

Works seamlessly when VoiceOver is on. Falls back to built-in speech synthesis when it's off. No configuration needed.

🌐

Works with any app

Pages, Google Docs, Word, your browser. If you can copy text from it, Math Recall can parse it.

⌨

Global hotkey

Option+Space activates from any application via the Accessibility API. No need to switch windows.

🔒

Fully local

All processing happens on your Mac. No cloud, no accounts, no data leaves your machine. Ever.

⚡

Smart input handling

Multi-digit buffering for large expressions. Tolerant navigation auto-skips single-item levels. It adapts to the equation.
