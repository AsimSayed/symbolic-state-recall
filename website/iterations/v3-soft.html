How it works

From flat audio
to structural navigation.

01 — The problem

Equations are walls, not windows

A sighted student glances at x² + 3x + 5 = 20 and instantly isolates the first term. A blind student hears the entire expression read aloud — start to finish, every time. For multi-step equations with dozens of terms, that means re-listening to everything just to find one piece. There's no way to jump ahead, no way to grab a sub-expression, and no way to insert it into your own work.

LINEAR · NO STRUCTURE · NO SELECTION
02 — The capture

Math Recall reads what you're working on

Copy an equation from your textbook, editor, or browser — or let Math Recall capture it directly from the focused app using macOS Accessibility APIs. Either way, the equation is instantly parsed into a tree structure you can navigate. Any equation that was flat audio is now explorable in as few as three keystrokes.

CLIPBOARD OR ACCESSIBILITY API → NAVIGABLE TREE
03 — The navigation

A simple grammar: line, side, term

Math Recall uses a consistent three-level grammar. Press a number to pick a line. Press L or R to choose a side of the equation. Press another number to select a term. At every step, VoiceOver announces where you are: "Left side, 3 items" → "x squared." You always know your position. You never lose your place.

NUMBER → SIDE → NUMBER · SPOKEN AT EVERY STEP
04 — The extraction

Space. Inserted. Done.

Press Space. The selected node is serialized back to plain text — x^2 — and inserted at your cursor in Notes.app. You never left your text editor. You never touched the clipboard. The entire interaction was non-visual, structural, and took under two seconds.

x^2 → CURSOR · ZERO VISION REQUIRED
What it parses

Calculus-level math.
From plain text.

No LaTeX required. No MathML. Math Recall accepts the kind of math notation people actually type. Because the people who need this tool shouldn't have to learn a second notation system to study the subject they already understand.

Expressions

Equations, powers, fractions

Full operator precedence handling, implicit multiplication, multi-line equation support with tolerant parsing.

x^2 + 3x = 7  •  (x+1)/(x-1)  •  3x → 3*x
Calculus

Integrals, derivatives, limits

Definite and indefinite integrals, derivative notation, limit expressions with approach syntax.

int_0^1 x^2 dx  •  d/dx  •  lim_x->0
Functions

Trig, logarithms, roots

sin, cos, tan, ln, log, square roots, nth roots. All parsed into navigable tree nodes.

sin(x)  •  ln(e^x)  •  sqrt(x)  •  root3(8)
Resilience

Error-tolerant by design

Skips non-math lines. Preserves last valid path on error. 400ms multi-digit input buffering. Never drops you out of recall mode.

// When your user can't see the screen, you don't crash.

About

Born from research.
Built at the University of Washington.

Math Recall started as We Are All Blind — an accessibility research project in HCDE 515 where we co-designed with a blind computer science student to understand how he actually experiences math. The finding was clear: the barrier isn't comprehension. It's memory.

“If a human scribe is a 10, this tool is like a 9 or 9.5.”
— Abdallah Altahaineh, design partner, after testing the prototype
